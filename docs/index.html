Weekly scores:

 

A two-dimensional plot for each game showing player predictions compared to the final result in red.
 
The grey star indicates the average of all predictions for a specific game.
 
Coloured bands show the expected bounds of alternative results based on dropped or gained scores for either team.
 
The Win/loss line indicates regions of phase space in which predictions correctly or incorrectly predicted the game winner.

Weekly rankings:

A ranking plot showing the variances and overall predictive success for each player for each game.
 
The blue (gold) bars indicate the difference between the home (away) prediction and the true result (P-T) normalised over the maximum difference for any player.
 
The metric of success for a prediction is the Try\({}^2\) value for which lower is better (think \(\chi^{2}\)). This is the total normalised difference from the final result reduced by a factor of 0.9 if the winner was correctly chosen.
 
The errors indicate \(1/\sigma^{avg}\), where \(\sigma^{avg}\) is the average variance for all predictions in the chosen game.

Tournament rankings:

Average ranking plot showing the mean variance and success for each player for each week, and over the full tournament.
 
The blue (gold) bars indicate the absolute mean difference between the home (away) prediction and the true result (|P-T|), shown un-normalised and with away results made negative for visualisation.
 
The Try\({}^2\) value is the average of all absolute difference from the final result mutliplied by the same factor of 0.9 for a correct win prediction.
 
The error bars show the standard error on the mean for each set of predictions.

Total statistical signficance:

Statistical significance of each player's predictions compared to random guesses, assessed per week, and over the full tournament.
 
A series of toy predictions are produced by picking a random score between 0 and 50 for each team.
 
The dashed line shows the mean of the normalised Try\({}^2\) distribution calculated for the ensemble of toy predictions, while the coloured bands indicate \(\pm\)1,2,3 standard deviations from the mean.
 
The points are each player's average Try\({}^2\) score, indicating the statistical significance of their predictions to not be randomly generated.
 
The dashed area indicates the statistical error from the limited number of toys, while the error bars show the standard error on the mean for each set of predictions.

Prediction bias:

Mean bias and over(under)-estimation percentage for each player towards each team.
 
The upper axes show each player's average bias towards the respective team, using a per-game metric:
 
(\(p_{n}\) - avg\({}_{n}\)) \(-\) (\(p_{o}\) - avg\({}_{o}\)) \(\times\) (\(N_{\text{diff}}\)/\(N_{\text{same}}\)) \(\times\) SF\({}_{\text{win}}\),
 
where \(p_{n(o)}\) is the predicted score for the given (other) team, avg\({}_{x}\) is the average of all player predictions, \(N_{\text{diff(same)}}\) is the number of players who predicted a different (same) match winner as the player, and SF\({}_{\text{win}}\) is an additional factor applied if a win is predicted.
 
The lower axes show the percentage over/underestimation of each team's performance based on the final results for each game.
 
For the absolute plot the difference in length of each set of axes indicates the overall strength of the bias.
